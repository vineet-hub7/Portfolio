Project 03 · RF / Analog

2G GSM Mobile Signal Jammer

900 MHz RF interference circuit using an NE555 astable multivibrator as the modulation source driving a BF495 RF transistor in common-emitter topology, with a tunable LC tank oscillator spanning 880–960 MHz.

Overview

This project demonstrates RF circuit fundamentals through the design of a 900 MHz interference generator targeting the GSM-900 band. The circuit combines analog oscillator design, transistor biasing, and impedance matching to produce RF output across the 2G cellular spectrum. The system employs a classic LC tank topology with air-core inductors and trimmer capacitors, allowing continuous frequency tuning across the target band via the LC resonance equation: f = 1/(2π√LC).

Key Components & Design

  • NE555 Astable Multivibrator: Generates the modulation/drive signal for the RF transistor. Frequency and duty cycle set by RC network.
  • BF495 RF Transistor: High-frequency transistor (fT ~3.5 GHz) configured in common-emitter topology for maximum gain at 900 MHz.
  • Common-Emitter Topology: Provides voltage and current gain, enabling efficient RF power amplification.
  • LC Tank Oscillator:
    • Air-core inductor: Lower loss, higher Q compared to ferrite
    • Trimmer capacitor: Variable tuning to sweep 880–960 MHz band
    • Resonance formula: f = 1 / (2π√LC)
  • Transistor Biasing: Q-point stabilized for linear operation, maximizing RF output power without entering saturation.

Analysis & Transistor Design

Q-Point Analysis

The transistor Q-point (quiescent operating point) defines DC operation and stability:

  • VCE (Collector-Emitter Voltage): Biased to mid-supply for maximum output swing without saturation.
  • IC (Collector Current): Set by base resistor network to ensure linear operation and prevent saturation/cutoff.
  • Thermal Stability: Resistor choices ensure Q-point remains stable with temperature variations.
  • RF Output Power: Maximized when transistor operates in linear region with minimal parasitic losses.

LC Tank Resonance

The parallel LC tank acts as the frequency-determining element. By adjusting the trimmer capacitor, the resonant frequency shifts according to:

fres = 1 / (2π√LC)

Air-core inductors minimize DC losses and parasitic resistance, maintaining high Q factor and enabling efficient power radiation.

Verification & Simulation

LTspice Simulation

The complete circuit was modeled and verified in LTspice:

  • Transient analysis to verify oscillation startup and stable operation
  • AC analysis to measure frequency response and peak power output
  • Parametric sweep of capacitor value to confirm 880–960 MHz tuning range
  • Biasing verification: VCE and IC within linear region
  • No saturation or cutoff observed across tuning range
